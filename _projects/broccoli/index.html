Overview

Problem

 COMMUNITY GARDENS IN NUMBERS

  • Over 6000 community gardens in the United States
  • 88 community gardens in the city of Seattle alone

 ISSUES FACED BY GARDENS

  • Unequal participation and sharing by community members
  • In Seattle, 1-2 year wait time to get plot in community garden

 WHY THIS IS A PROBLEM

  • 14% of American households have limited access to fresh food
  • Fresh produce can be far away or too expensive

Our Solution

To allow any passerby regardless of economic status to become involved with the garden, a tablet interface would now sit at the garden entrance. Gardeners we spoke with have difficulty getting started and learning new skills. With "Broccoli for All," citizens can join a community garden to learn alongside their neighbors in the pursuit of a common goal. Once a garden member, they can consult a companion mobile app to receive notifications about the garden.

Get acquainted with your neighborhood garden. As a garden manager, more easily complete existing tasks of monitoring plot health and notifying gardeners of updates.

View overall garden health. Click on your plot to see how it is doing. Help ensure that all gardeners are putting forth the needed effort to keep the garden healthy and prevent collective neglect of plots.

Apply to volunteer on another individual's plot rather than wait a couple years to obtain your own. Work with your co-gardener, learn valuable agricutlural skills along the way.
